EXIT STORIES
CREATIVITY
A man and I once took his 10 year old sister to New York MoMA. He turned his nose up at the minimalist exhibit and told the girl that it wasn't art. I was mesmerized by a video of a woman lying on a spiral of slowly unraveling watermelons that floated in the Caspian Sea. I told the man's sister that it's art if it does something to your brain. I still believe that.
COLLABORATION
The best class I ever took was called Direction, Design, and Choreography at NYU.
We choreographers collaborated with set designers, lighting designers, costume designers, dancers, and composers.
Since then, I've collaborated with programmers, video artists, musicians, producers, sculptors, stylists, actors, directors, animators, and graphic designers.

COMMUNITY
A wise man told me recently, when you say 'yes' to the world, the world says 'yes' to you. I am dedicated to building one free webpage a month for artists and community organizations. Ask to get on the calendar!
LEADERSHIP
The first time I led a group, I taught middle school kids how to build fires and shelters.
The second time, I led a summer dance program.
Nobody ever told me that leadership was supposed to be difficult.
So I directed a dance company for four years. This was easy, because dancers are disciplined. It was hard because I tried to do too many things. I learned to delegate.
When I decided to lead bands and record albums, I was surprised. Musicians are not all disciplined. I learned to read people and work with the best.
Now I always work with the best. Leadership is easy with great teams.
PROGRAMMING
In high school, my father told me one day that he had decided what my college major was going to be. My mother and I admonished him for deciding my future for me, but asked what it was. He said software engineering.
I rebelled against my Dad for years. A couple years ago, I realized what a cool idea he had, and that he understood all those years ago how my brain works. So I became a software engineer.
